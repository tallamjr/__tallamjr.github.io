About Me

I am a person who is fascinated with the world around me and really enjoy learning how things work at a fundamental level. After graduating from Royal Holloway with a MSci in Astrophysics, I knew I wanted to continue on a path towards research. During my time at Royal Holloway I discovered a passion for using scientific computing to solve problems, so following my undergraduate masters I decided to develop my skills further by enrolling in a post-graduate masters in Computer Science at UCL.

I was lucky enough to be able to do my major project that combined many areas of research I am interessted about and to work with cutting edge technologies. My aim now is to continue developing my research skills ultimately leading to a PhD in the fields of Astrophysics, Astroinformatics.

Eventhough I spend much of my time learning about applied mathematical conecpts and scientitific computing to solve research probelms, I also enjoy studying and programming as a hobby. A collection of topics I like to dabble in can be found in my blog where I cover all sorts from my data science projects on kaggle to awesome vim and UNIX tricks.
